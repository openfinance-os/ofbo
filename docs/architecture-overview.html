Executive summary

Open Finance is the example. The model is the deliverable.

The headline is not the product; it is the method. In building the Open Finance Back Office (OFBO) we created — and proved — a repeatable way for the bank to take any regulated solution from idea to live, audit-ready software. Open Finance is simply the first domain we ran through it; the same form factor applies to payments, lending, onboarding, servicing, risk and beyond — any solution, any domain. What turns this generic model into a given institution's own way of building is the context it accumulates — its regulated-entity brain (section 3).

The form factor, not the product: one model on the left, many domains on the right. Open Finance proved the line.

The model is a double diamond: a discovery harness that turns an ambiguous challenge into an agreed, evidence-backed problem statement (the PRD), and a delivery harness that turns that PRD into shipped software with regulatory controls enforced automatically at every step. That combination buys speed with control — every change is specification-first, four-eyes approved, and auto-deployed to a live demo; nothing ships without passing build, security, integration and BCBS 239 data-lineage gates; and no real customer data ever touches the system. Software is demonstrable from day one and audit-ready by construction, not by remediation.

Read this as a blueprint, not a case study. Part A is the approach — the form factor any team can adopt. Part B is the system it produced, as concrete proof the model works end to end. It closes with how the bank builds from here.

Part A · How we built it — the approach How an idea becomes shipped software here: two diamonds — first discover the right problem, then deliver it under automated control.

1The double diamond — our operating model

The build harness below is the delivery half of the process — it assumes you already know what to build (the PRD). Discovery is the half that produces that PRD. Framed against the Design Council's Double Diamond, the whole flow is two diamonds: Discover → Define (the problem), then Develop → Deliver (the solution). Each diamond diverges to explore, then converges to decide; they meet at the agreed problem statement / PRD — the output of discovery and the canon of delivery. It is non-linear: evidence found late can send you back a diamond.

Discovery harness (Diamond 1) — from challenge to PRD

One discovery per opportunity, evidence-first, with a kill-switch:

  • ① Frame the challenge — strategy/OKRs, a regulatory mandate, a customer/market signal, an exec sponsor ask, or a pain point/incident.
  • ② Discover (diverge) — gather evidence, not solutions: stakeholder & user research, market & competitor scan, regulatory & risk landscape (discovery-mode risk review), data & signal analysis, current-state journey mapping.
  • ③ Define (converge) — synthesize evidence into insight themes, frame the problem ("How might we…"), build the business case (NPV/IRR, cost-benefit, options), prioritise & scope, draft the problem statement.
  • ⌥ Optional RFP branch (build-vs-buy) — when discovery surfaces a capability gap: RFI → RFP → scored (weighted) evaluation → shortlist + PoC/demo → partner selection; vendor-shaped constraints fold back into the problem statement.
  • ④ Gate — investment committee approval + problem-statement sign-off. The four-eyes of discovery; kill-criteria can stop here (a discovery is allowed to fail).
  • ⑤ Handoff — the agreed problem statement becomes the PRD + acceptance criteria (plus any selected partner) and seeds the delivery canon (CLAUDE.md · PRD · OpenAPI · backlog.yaml).

2Delivery harness (Diamond 2) — how OFBO is built end-to-end

The repo is built by an AI-DLC story loop on top of a quality-gated CI/CD pipeline. Canon is CLAUDE.md + the PRD + specs/backoffice-openapi.yaml + docs/backlog.yaml (BACKOFFICE-01..80). One story per branch, spec-first, demoable on merge.

Authoring guardrails (enforced)

.claude/settings.json sets worktree.bgIsolation="worktree" (background sessions are hard-blocked from editing the main checkout until EnterWorktree). Hooks in .claude/hooks/ run on every session and Edit/Write: worktree-policy (SessionStart reminder), pii-guard (blocks 784… Emirates IDs / real-shaped IBANs), and spec-tripwire (locks specs/backoffice-openapi.yaml mid-feature — the contract changes only via a dedicated spec PR). Pre-PR, two subagents review the diff: contract-conformance-reviewer (spec fidelity) and hard-stop-reviewer (scope matrix, audit immutability, PII, egress, four-eyes, profile branching).

3The regulated-entity brain — training the model on the institution

A regulated entity is not generic, so the model cannot be either. What makes the Loom produce this institution’s software is context — a regulated-entity brain the harnesses read from at every step and write back to every cycle. It has a general-practice base that ships with the Loom, and on top of it the three dimensions that are the institution’s own: regulated context (the regime’s rules), the solution domain (this problem space), and institutional DNA (how the bank itself works). Context is king; this is where it lives.

How the brain is built

  • Seeded from what the entity already has — policies & standards, governance & org structure, glossaries & house style, regulator mandates, system & vendor inventory, and the record of past decisions.
  • Two layers. General practice ships with the model (conventions, security posture, and regulatory patterns such as four-eyes, INSERT-only audit and data lineage). Entity-specific context is built up over time across the wider spectrum — how we work (ways of working, governance & approvals, architectural patterns, decisions/ADRs), what we build with (approved technologies, code repos & reference implementations, design system & tokens, platform/infra blueprints), how we communicate (terminology & wording, abbreviations & acronyms, comms style & channels, brand & tone), and what's regulated & at stake (regulatory specifics, systems & ports mapping, personas & scopes, risk taxonomy & controls).
  • One governed home. The brain is versioned in the repository — binding conventions (CLAUDE.md), a memory store, decisions (ADRs), and the entity's port-mapping configuration — a single source of truth, not tribal knowledge in people's heads.
  • Compounds every cycle. Each discovery and delivery deposits new context, so onboarding the next solution is faster, safer, and more clearly the entity's own.

For leadership this is the moat. The model is generic by design and therefore copyable; the trained brain is not. It is where general best practice and the institution's own identity meet — and it is what turns "a way to build" into this regulated entity's way of building.

Part B · What we built — the system architecture The system the approach produced: a vendor-neutral, audit-ready Open Finance back office.

4System context — who talks to what

Two ingresses reach the BFF Worker (admin-scoped): the operator portal (humans) and an agents interface — services/mcp-gateway (@ofbo/mcp-gateway, ADR 0017), an experimental agent-first surface that turns each @ofbo/contracts route into an MCP tool. It is a pure contract client: it adds no enforcement primitive of its own — auth, RBAC, four-eyes (202 + approval_request), INSERT-only audit and lineage all stay BFF-side (defence in depth). It is read-only by default; mutating tools stay gated on BACKOFFICE-53 spend-control plus a human raising a persona’s budget, and agent identities are issued via DCR (BACKOFFICE-60, ADR 0018). The BFF’s own agents module is the authoritative persona catalogue; the gateway is not in the deploy pipeline. The application core is DEPLOY_PROFILE-agnostic; shared packages and the simulated externals (Nebras, Supabase, Cloudflare) sit underneath, and all Nebras-bound traffic leaves only through P6 egress.

9Worked example — fraud-suspected revoke (four-eyes)

BACKOFFICE-22: a Risk analyst posts a fraud revocation; the BFF requests a four-eyes approval and returns 202 + approval_request instead of acting inline. A second Risk approver approves, the BFF revokes through P6 egress with reason FRAUD_SUSPECTED, gets a sub-5s acknowledgement, writes a consent_revoked event to the High-class audit trail with an STR draft reference, and returns 200. PSU notification is deferred per fraud policy; STR submission is out of scope (BACKOFFICE-63).

How we build, going forward

The strategic point is simple: Open Finance was the proving ground; the model is what scales. What we validated on one domain is a general-purpose way for the bank to build — solution-agnostic and domain-agnostic. The next initiative does not start from a blank page; it starts by running the same two harnesses.

The discovery harness gives leadership a disciplined funnel — frame the challenge, gather evidence, build the business case, then a gated go/no-go (with an RFP branch when it is a build-vs-buy decision) — so we fund the right problems and stop the wrong ones early and cheaply. The delivery harness gives engineering a factory where compliance, security and auditability are properties of the pipeline, not afterthoughts bolted on before an audit. Because every external system is a port and every control is a gate, the same machine adapts: a new product, a new domain, a new regulator, or a new vendor reuses seams that already exist. Scaling means adding stories to the line — not rebuilding the line.

What this unlocks for the bank:

  • One operating model, many domains — payments, lending, onboarding, servicing, risk: each reuses the same discovery-to-delivery machine instead of reinventing one.
  • Faster time-to-evidence — a working, demoable slice early, so decisions are made on something real.
  • Predictable, auditable delivery — every release carries its own quality and lineage evidence; compliance is by construction.
  • Lower key-person risk — the process lives in the repo (conventions, gates, hooks, reviewers), not in people's heads.

Open Finance Back Office is the first thing we built this way. It should not be the last — it should be the template.
